Letter from the CEO

Dear Shareholders,

2025 has been a year of extraordinary volatility for NovaEnergy Corporation, reflecting the broader turbulence in global energy markets. We experienced dramatic swings in renewable energy demand, regulatory environments, and commodity prices that both challenged our operations and created opportunities for strategic growth.

Our financial results tell the story of a transformative but tumultuous year. Revenue increased by 28.5% to $7.4 billion, driven primarily by the acquisition of GreenStream Technologies and unprecedented demand for our utility-scale battery storage solutions. However, our operating income declined by 35.2% to $612 million, impacted by supply chain disruptions, extreme weather events affecting our renewable installations, and integration costs from our recent acquisitions.

Despite these challenges, we achieved several significant milestones. Our installed renewable capacity increased by 42% to 14.8 GW, with particularly strong growth in solar and offshore wind. We secured major contracts in emerging markets, expanding our global footprint to 28 countries. Our R&D investments in next-generation storage technologies and smart grid solutions have strengthened our long-term competitive position.

Looking ahead to 2026, we anticipate continued volatility in energy markets but remain confident in our strategic direction. The global energy transition is accelerating, creating substantial opportunities for companies with our capabilities and vision. We will continue to focus on operational excellence, disciplined capital allocation, and strategic investments in high-growth areas of the evolving energy landscape.

I thank our shareholders for their continued support during this dynamic period and our employees for their remarkable resilience and innovation in the face of unprecedented change.

Dr. Amara Wilson
Chief Executive Officer and Chairperson

Acquisition of GreenStream Technologies

On July 15, 2025, NovaEnergy completed the acquisition of GreenStream Technologies, a leader in advanced grid management software and virtual power plant solutions, for $1.68 billion. This strategic acquisition has:

  • Added proprietary AI-driven software that optimizes renewable energy integration into existing grids
  • Expanded our customer base by over 200 utilities and large commercial clients
  • Contributed approximately $475 million in revenue and $68 million in operating income in H2 2025
  • Created significant integration challenges that impacted short-term financial performance
  • Positioned us to capitalize on the growing grid modernization market, expected to reach $160 billion globally by 2030

Integration costs totaled $137 million in 2025, with additional costs of approximately $75-90 million expected in 2026.

Market Volatility Impact

The renewable energy sector experienced unprecedented volatility in 2025, affecting our financial performance in several ways:

  • Commodity Price Fluctuations: Battery material prices fluctuated by up to 65% within the fiscal year, compressing margins in our Energy Storage segment.
  • Regulatory Changes: Sudden shifts in renewable energy incentives in key European markets created both challenges and opportunities.
  • Extreme Weather Events: Category 4 Hurricane Orion in October 2025 damaged our Gulf Coast facilities, resulting in $165 million in impairment charges and operational disruptions.
  • Geopolitical Tensions: Supply chain disruptions due to international conflicts affected component availability and pricing.
  • Interest Rate Volatility: Rapidly changing interest rate environments affected project financing costs and valuation of long-term contracts.

Outlook for 2026

Given the volatile nature of our industry and recent performance, we are providing wider guidance ranges than in previous years:

  • Revenue: $8.2-9.0 billion (11-21% growth)
  • Operating Income: $750-950 million (23-55% growth)
  • EBITDA: $1.45-1.65 billion (14-30% growth)
  • EPS: $2.80-3.40 (21-47% growth)
  • Capital Expenditures: $1.8-2.1 billion
  • Free Cash Flow: $0-200 million (vs. -$212 million in 2025)
  • New Capacity Additions: 3.8-4.5 GW

This outlook assumes:

  • Gradually stabilizing energy markets
  • Successful integration of GreenStream Technologies
  • Continued but moderating supply chain challenges
  • Average weather conditions (without the extreme events experienced in 2025)
  • No significant changes to renewable energy incentives in key markets
