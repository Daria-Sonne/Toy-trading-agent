Letter from the CEO

Dear Shareholders,

2025 has been a challenging year for UrbanMart Retail Group as we continue to navigate the highly competitive retail landscape and shifting consumer behaviors. Our financial performance reflects the ongoing challenges in the sector, with revenue declining 6.4% to $32.7 billion and comparable store sales decreasing by 4.8%.

Despite these challenges, we have made progress on our transformation strategy. We have closed 87 underperforming stores, accelerated our e-commerce investments, and implemented cost-cutting measures that have reduced our operating expenses by 2.1%. Our newly launched loyalty program has shown promising early results, with membership growing to 14.3 million customers since its introduction in Q2.

We recognize that our turnaround will take time and additional strategic initiatives. To this end, we have secured $750 million in new financing to strengthen our balance sheet and provide the capital needed to accelerate our digital transformation and store revitalization program. We have also expanded our partnership with leading brands to enhance our product assortment and improve gross margins.

Looking ahead, we remain committed to returning UrbanMart to profitable growth through our three strategic pillars: optimizing our store network, enhancing our omnichannel capabilities, and improving operational efficiency. While we anticipate continued headwinds in 2026, we believe these foundational changes will position us for long-term success in the evolving retail environment.

Thank you for your continued support and patience as we work diligently to transform UrbanMart and create sustainable value for our shareholders.

Michael J. Reynolds
Chief Executive Officer

Going Concern and Debt Covenant Compliance

Management has evaluated the company's ability to continue as a going concern and acknowledges the significant challenges facing the business, including:

  • Consecutive years of operating losses
  • Declining comparable store sales
  • Competitive pressures in the retail sector
  • Increasing debt levels

However, management believes that the Company has sufficient liquidity to meet its obligations for at least the next 12 months based on:

  • The successful $750 million debt refinancing completed in Q3 2025
  • Cost reduction initiatives that are expected to yield $350 million in annual savings
  • The planned divestiture of non-core assets estimated to generate $425 million in proceeds
  • Positive cash flow from operating activities of $1.2 billion in FY 2025

As of December 31, 2025, the Company was in compliance with all debt covenants, but notes that continued deterioration in performance could result in potential covenant violations in the latter half of 2026. Management is proactively working with lenders to address this possibility.

Outlook for 2026

Based on current market conditions and the execution of our transformation strategy, we provide the following guidance for fiscal year 2026:

  • Revenue is expected to decline 2-4% due to planned store closures, partially offset by growth in e-commerce and improved performance in renovated stores
  • Comparable store sales expected to improve to -2% to 0%, with sequential improvement throughout the year
  • Gross margin expected to increase 40-60 basis points through improved inventory management and product mix
  • Plan to close an additional 120-150 underperforming stores
  • Capital expenditures of $450-500 million, primarily focused on e-commerce capabilities, supply chain optimization, and strategic store remodels
  • Target of achieving break-even operating income by Q4 2026

Transformation Strategy

Our three-year transformation strategy is focused on the following key initiatives:

  1. Store Network Optimization
    • Close 250-300 underperforming stores by the end of 2027
    • Remodel 200 high-potential locations with enhanced layouts and digital integration
    • Reduce average store size by 15% to improve productivity and reduce operating costs
  2. Digital Transformation
    • Target e-commerce to reach 25% of total sales by 2027
    • Enhance mobile app functionality and personalization capabilities
    • Upgrade fulfillment infrastructure to support same-day delivery in all major markets
    • Implement AI-driven inventory management and demand forecasting
  3. Operational Efficiency
    • Implement $650 million in cost savings by 2027
    • Streamline corporate organizational structure
    • Optimize supply chain and logistics operations
    • Reduce SKU count by 15% while enhancing core product categories
  4. Financial Stability
    • Reduce total debt by $800 million by 2027
    • Divest non-core assets
    • Restore positive operating income by 2026
    • Return to sustainable profitability by 2027
