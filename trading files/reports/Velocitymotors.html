Letter from the CEO

Dear Shareholders,

2025 has been a year of steady performance for VelocityMotors Corporation amid a challenging automotive landscape characterized by supply chain stabilization, shifting consumer preferences, and ongoing electrification of our industry. Our revenue reached $92.3 billion, representing a modest 2.8% increase year-over-year, while we maintained a healthy operating margin of 8.4%.

We continued to execute our balanced approach to growth, focusing on operational excellence, product innovation, and strategic investments in electric and autonomous vehicle technologies. Our new Velocity EV-8 crossover has been well-received by consumers, contributing to a 15.3% increase in our electric vehicle sales, which now represent 22% of our total sales volume.

Despite persistent semiconductor shortages in the first half of the year, our supply chain resilience initiatives allowed us to minimize production disruptions and maintain our market position in key regions. Our commitment to cost discipline and operational efficiency generated $1.2 billion in cost savings, helping us offset inflationary pressures and invest in critical future technologies.

Looking ahead to 2026, we remain cautiously optimistic about the automotive market. While we anticipate continued macroeconomic uncertainties, we are well-positioned with a balanced product portfolio, a strong balance sheet, and a clear strategy focused on gradual electrification, digitalization, and enhanced customer experience. We will continue to prudently invest in our future while delivering consistent results for our shareholders.

Thank you for your continued support and confidence in VelocityMotors.

James R. Mitchell
Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

Top-Selling Models

Velocity Cruiser SUV

Units Sold: 487,300 (FY 2025) vs 462,500 (FY 2024) +5.4%

Our flagship mid-size SUV continued to lead sales with strong performance in North America and Asia. The newly refreshed model featuring advanced driver assistance systems and improved fuel efficiency has been well-received by consumers.

Velocity Titan Pickup

Units Sold: 412,800 (FY 2025) vs 387,400 (FY 2024) +6.6%

The Titan pickup truck series maintained its strong market position with enhanced towing capacity and new technology features. Commercial fleet sales were particularly strong in North America.

Velocity Sentinel Sedan

Units Sold: 378,600 (FY 2025) vs 412,300 (FY 2024) -8.2%

Our traditional sedan saw declining sales as consumer preferences continued to shift toward SUVs and crossovers. However, it remained popular in Asian markets and with fleet customers.

Velocity EV-8 Crossover

Units Sold: 312,500 (FY 2025) vs 251,800 (FY 2024) +24.1%

Our newest electric crossover showed impressive growth with its 350-mile range and fast-charging capabilities. Strong demand in Europe and China drove sales performance.

Velocity Compact

Units Sold: 294,700 (FY 2025) vs 308,200 (FY 2024) -4.4%

Our entry-level compact car saw a moderate decline as competition intensified in the segment, particularly from new low-cost electric vehicles in key markets.

Electric and Autonomous Vehicle Investments

In 2025, we continued to accelerate our investments in electric and autonomous vehicle technologies:

  • Invested $2.8 billion in electric vehicle development and production capacity
  • Expanded our EV portfolio to 12 models, up from 9 in 2024
  • Increased battery production capacity by 35% year-over-year
  • Formed strategic partnerships with two leading battery technology companies
  • Advanced our autonomous driving technology with the release of Highway Pilot 3.0
  • Expanded our charging network to over 15,000 stations globally, a 42% increase from 2024
  • Committed to investing $18 billion in EV and autonomous technologies over the next 5 years

Outlook for 2026

Based on current market conditions and our operational plans, we provide the following guidance for fiscal year 2026:

  • Revenue growth of 2-4% year-over-year
  • Vehicle deliveries growth of 1.5-3.0% year-over-year
  • Operating margin of 8.3-8.7%
  • Electric vehicle sales to represent 25-27% of total vehicle sales
  • Capital expenditures of $6.6-6.9 billion
  • R&D investments of $4.7-5.0 billion, with continued focus on electrification and autonomous technologies
  • Continued commitment to shareholder returns through dividends and share repurchases
  • Launch of 3 new electric models and 2 refreshed conventional models

Risk Factors

Key risks that may affect our future results include:

  • Macroeconomic Uncertainty: Inflation, interest rates, and consumer confidence may impact vehicle demand.
  • Supply Chain Challenges: While improving, semiconductor and other component availability continues to present risks.
  • Regulatory Environment: Evolving emissions and safety regulations may require additional investments.
  • Competitive Intensity: Increased competition in the EV space from both traditional and new market entrants.
  • Technology Transition: The pace of EV adoption may vary across markets, affecting our product mix and profitability.
  • Currency Fluctuations: Foreign exchange volatility may impact our international operations and financial results.
  • Trade Policies: Changes in tariffs and trade agreements could affect our global manufacturing and supply chain strategy.
